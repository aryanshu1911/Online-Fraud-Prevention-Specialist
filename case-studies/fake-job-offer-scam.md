# 💸 FAKE JOB OFFER SCAM: Bengaluru, India (2025)

## 📍 Overview

In 2025, a job seeker from **Bengaluru, India (Manjunath S)** fell victim to a fake job offer scam after receiving a seemingly legitimate opportunity through an online job platform. He went through multiple interview rounds and eventually resigned from his existing job, only to later discover that the company and job offer did not exist. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/combating-job-scams-indias-digital-market-2025-rahul-bhoyar-qfugc)

In India, job scams on platforms like Naukri.com and Shine.com increased by **101% in 2024**, with victims losing between **₹1,800 to ₹2,00,000** per case. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/combating-job-scams-indias-digital-market-2025-rahul-bhoyar-qfugc)

***

## 📍 What Happened — Timeline of Events

**Fake Job Posting Appears:**
A fraudulent job listing was posted on a legitimate job platform with attractive salary and benefits.

**Application & Screening:**
The victim applied and was shortlisted, receiving professional communication from fake HR representatives.

**Multiple Interview Rounds:**
The victim went through **three rounds of interviews**, increasing trust in the legitimacy of the offer.

**Job Offer Received:**
A formal offer letter was issued, convincing the victim that the job was real.

**Resignation from Current Job:**
Based on the offer, the victim resigned from his existing job and prepared to join.

**Job Disappears:**
On the joining date, the company and HR contacts became unreachable. The job offer turned out to be fake.

**Realization of Fraud:**
The victim realized he had been scammed and was left unemployed without income. [linkedin](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/combating-job-scams-indias-digital-market-2025-rahul-bhoyar-qfugc)

***

## 🧠 Why This Was a Fake Job Offer Scam

| Characteristic | How It Matched |
|---|---|
| Fake Recruitment Process | Entire hiring pipeline was fabricated |
| Multiple Interviews | Created false sense of legitimacy |
| Fake Offer Letter | Used official-looking documents to build trust |
| No Real Company | Employer disappeared on joining date |
| Career Disruption | Victim resigned before verification |

***

## 🔎 What Made This Scam Work

| Factor | Why It Worked |
|---|---|
| Legitimate Job Platforms | Posted on trusted portals like Naukri.com |
| Professional Communication | Fake HR used formal interview process |
| Psychological Trust Building | Multiple interviews created credibility |
| Urgency & Opportunity | Attractive salary pressured quick decision-making |
| Lack of Verification | Victim did not independently verify company existence |

***

## 🎯 Key Lessons

- Never resign before verifying the employer independently
- Always check company registration and official website
- Verify HR email domains (avoid Gmail/unknown domains)
- Cross-check company on LinkedIn and government registries
- Treat unsolicited job offers with caution, even from job portals

***

## 🚨 Key Insight

A fake job offer scam is not just a financial fraud—it can directly destroy careers by forcing victims to **self-trigger their own unemployment** through misplaced trust.
